Six-rotor multi-copter offering high redundancy — the drone continues to fly even after one motor failure. Preferred for professional cinematography and heavy-lift payload missions.

Six equally-spaced arms radiating from central hub. Larger frame than a quadcopter. Often has landing gear beneath the body.

OCT-8

Octacopter

ROTORS8
CONFIGSymmetric

Eight-rotor powerhouse designed for maximum payload capacity and fault tolerance. Widely used in film production, surveying, and industrial inspection where downtime is unacceptable.

Max Payload Industrial Film

Eight arms at 45° intervals from central hub. Bulkier and wider than hexacopters. Often coaxial (stacked motor pairs) in compact variants.

The most ubiquitous drone form factor. Simple mechanics, mature software ecosystems, and a balanced trade-off between cost, agility, and flight time make it the go-to consumer UAV.

Consumer Photography Racing

Four arms forming an X or + pattern. Equal arm length. Two pairs of counter-rotating props. Most recognisable drone silhouette.

Single large main rotor with elongated body (fuselage) and a small anti-torque tail rotor. Distinctly helicopter-like silhouette.

Three-rotor Y-frame design. Yaw control is achieved by tilting one rear motor via a servo, producing a uniquely agile and smooth yaw response favoured by FPV pilots and videographers.

Three arms in a Y pattern — two forward arms and one rear. The rear motor mount visibly tilts. Smaller and lighter than a quad.
